Ending Nigeria herder-farmer crisis: Di Livestock Reform Plan
Nigeria federal and state goment dey struggle to implement National Livestock Transformation Plan wey dey seek to curb di movement of cattle across di kontri and reduce deadly herder-farmer conflict
While di Plan don earn di endorsement of plenti state goment, e dey face significant challenges
Deficient political leadership, popular misperceptions about hin purpose, budgetary constraints wey di COVID-19 pandemic aggravated, lack of personnel wey get di expertise to carry am out and widespread insecurity, dey all di insecurity wey dey stop progress
If di Plan fail as previous initiative to modern livestock management don do, herder-farmer violence go fit escalate
Di National Economic Council approve am for January 2019, di Plan represent di most comprehensive effort wey dem go use overhaul Nigeria inefficient and grossly underperforming livestock system
At di core na strategy wey go curtail migratory or open grazing, and thus, lower di risk of conflict wey dey between herder dem and farmers
E dey animated by di hope say over 10 years, predominantly nomadic pastoralists go be persuaded to move dia cattle into ranches and public grazing reserves, wia breedingg farms and oda mechanised livestock management practices na to boost di sector productivity.
As di end of 2028 dey end, authorities go need get at least 119 ranches wey dey operate for all di participatin states, wey go create more dan two million jobs for di livestock production, processin and marketing chains.
President Muhammadu Buhari administration kom up wit di Plan afta wave of violence wey dey between herder dem and farmers, particularly a surge in related violence wey dey afflict rural dwellers for 2018
Di federal goment, wey don commit to funding 80 per cent of di transformation proposal wey di participatin states submit, don take preliminary steps toward putting di Plan into practice, wey include providing technical support wey go help states prepare for implementation, like to dey help dem do field survey and site mapping.
Plenty states don reactivate earlier demarcated grazing reserves, dey open offices and set up steering committees wey go administer di Plan
Authorities don also do workshops and done oda works wey go explain di benefits of livestock reform to a large audience
But two years enta di 10-year plan, di first new ranch wey dem neva build yet, and di obstacles plenti
Opposition, partly among herder dem, predominantly from di Fulani ethnic group but also from farmers wey resent di Plans benefits to pastoralists, e dey hinders reform efforts.
Distrust dey widespread for di Middle Belt, wey don be di scene of violent dispute ova land between herders and farmers, even witstandin peacemaking and military effort wey since 2018 don reduce di bloodshed
Federal and state budget dey pinched, partly due to di pandemic economic fallout, and di kontri lack sufficient technical expertise wey dem go use manage ranches and reserves.
For many states, especially for di North-West, di proliferation of criminal gangs and oda armed groups dey cut off access to grazing reserves and dey scare away potential investors
Unless dem don address dis and oda problem, e fit delay or even scuttle di Plan, e fit leave di kontri vulnerable to an escalation of herder-farmer conflict, wey fit degenerate into wider ethnic, regional and religious violence.
To tackle dis challenges requires concerted effort by federal and state authorities, wit help from donors and investors.
First, Abuja and di state goment wey dey support be say make dem provide stronger political leadership and improve public communication so dem go fit avoid misgivin and misperceptions about di Plan.
Dis dey especially prevalent among pastoralists, wey go change a centuries-old nomadic lifestyle, and wey go legitimately doubt di Plans promises about available pasture.
But many farmers dey fearful too, say dem fit lose land to livestock producers.
Many pipo for di Middle Belt and Southern states dey deeply suspicious of di Plan long-term goals, wey dem see as creating privileges for herders, and more broadly, di Fulani
wit Abuja and oda partners support, state goment must build expertise and technical capacity, especially for ranches and grazing reserves, dairy production and meat processin.
Federal and state goment make dem up budgetary commitments, make dem hasten di release of funds and provide financial transparency to ensure accountability, working wit donors and investors as necessary.
To stop rural insecurity, curbing impunity and to rehabilitate communities wey don affect earlier violence for participatin states dey also important
plus, authorities go need to consider climate changes likely impact and reach decision about how di transhumant herders wey dey cross into Nigeria seasonally should or should not benefit from di Plan.
As some of dis steps go take time, di Plan proponents suppose focus on to deliver concrete, visible result for short-term.
less dan two years, di kontri go do general election
If di Plan na to survive di change of goment wey go follow, politicians go need to produce clear proof of im benefits (If im don serve two terms, President Buhari and plenti state governors no go fit compete, wey mean say change dey sure.)
By di time wey dem dey campaign heat up for late 2022, di backers of Plan suppose dey able to point at least one handful of newly constructed ranches or rehabilitated reserves, strong donor and investor commitments, and di first of a cadre of newly trained livestock management professionals.
None of dis go dey easy, but di Plan dey worth di effort
As e no dey perfect, e dey offer important chance to reform Nigeria livestock system wit strategy wey go address di need of herder and small-scale farmers.

E-commerce for Nigeria: Legal framework, challenges and prospect
E-commerce dey refer to di use of communications technology particularly di internet to buy, sell and market goods and services to customers
Di internet don bring about a fundamental shift for national economies wey dey isolated from each oda by barriers to cross-border trade and investment,isolated by distance, time zone and languages and isolated by di national differences for goment regulations
E-commerce sef dey foster direct access to distant markets and e dey promote globalisation and commercial activities and e dey blur plenti of di current distinction wey dey between domestic and foreign companies to an extent saye practically impossible to determine di origin of di products
E-commerce no dey limited to di purchase of product, e include e-mail, and oda communication platforms, all information or service wey a company fit offer to customers over di net, from pre-purchase information to aftersale service and support
Di major significance of E-commerce na di fact say e dey encourage single world trading system, wey access through electronic means to goods and services from different parts of di world
Most of di statues wey dey exist for Nigeria don lag backward towards di development and growth of e-commerce in particular and ICT as a whole
Plenti legal research and study dem don do on e-commerce for Nigeria
Most of dis studies dey of di opinion say di Nigerian legal system dey lay behind ICT-based legislation and say di existing statutory laws dey quite inadequate to address some of di legal issues wey dey affect e-commerce
Dis issue include electronic evidence cybercrimes, security and data production, e-payment system and inevitably trans-border issues, and many more.
Need dey, therefore, to map out appropriate legal and regulatory measures, wey go govern di emerging regime
Dey don state am plenti time say for Nigeria, significant efforts for di regulation of e-commerce activities dey still for di stage of draft Bills before di National Assembly.

Di courts fit also adapt to recognise electronic transactions in di absence of legislation or private contract.

We dey hope say oda Nigerian judges go adopt dis judicial activism, Supreme Court justices go inclusive, especially for di areas wia awa current legislation dey inadequate, comprehensive or elaborate enough to address legal challenges of di emerging technological advancements.
