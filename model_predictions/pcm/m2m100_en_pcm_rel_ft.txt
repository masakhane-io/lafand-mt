Endin Nigeria herder-farmer crisis: di Livestock Reform Plan
Nigeria federal and state goment dey struggle to implement a National Livestock Transformation Plan wey dey find to stop di movement of cattle across di kontri and reduce herder-farmer conflict wey dey deadly.
While di plan don earn di endorsement of plenti state goment, e dey face significant challenges.
Deficient political leadership, popular misperceptions about hin purpose, budgetary constraints aggravated by di COVID-19 pandemic, lack of personnel wit di expertise to carry am out and widespread insecurity, dey hinder progress
If di plan fail as previous initiative to modernise livestock management do, herder-farmer violence fit escalate
National Economic Council approve am for January 2019, di plan represent di effort wey dey comprehensive pass to overhaul Nigeria inefficient and grossly underperforming livestock system.
For di core na strategy to reduce di migration or open grazing, and thus, reduce di risk of conflict between herder dem and farmer dem.
E dey animated by di hope say ova 10 years, predominantly nomadic pastoralists go dey persuaded to move dia cattle into ranches and public grazing reserves, wia breedingg farms and oda mechanized livestock management practices na to boost di sector productivity.
By di end of 2028, authorities dey aim to get at least 119 ranches wey dey operate for all participatin states, wey go create ova two million jobs for livestock production, processin and marketing chains.
President Muhammadu Buhari administration kom up wit di plan afta a wave of violence wey dey between herders and farmers, a surge for related violence wey dey afflict rural dwellers for 2018
Di federal goment, wey don committed to fund 80 per cent of di transformation proposals wey participatin states submit, don take preliminary steps toward puttin di Plan into practice, including providing technical support wey go help states prepare for implementation, like help wit field surveys and site maping.
Plenty states don reactivate earlier demarcated grazing reserves, open offices and set up steering committee to administer di Plan.
Authorities don also do workshops and do oda work to explain di benefits of livestock reform to large audience
But two years inside di 10-year plan, dem neva build di first new ranch, and di obstacles dey plenti
Di oposition, partly among herder dem, predominantly from di Fulani ethnic group but also among farmers wey dey resent di plan benefit to pastoralists, dey hindre reform efforts.
Distrust dey widespread for di Middle Belt, wey don be di scene of violent disputes ova land between herders and farmers, notwithstanding peacemaking and military efforts wey since 2018 don reduce di bloodshed.
Dem pinch federal and state budget, partly bikos of di pandemics economic fallout, and di kontri lack technical expertise wey dey sufficient for managing ranches and grazing reserves
For plenti state, especially for di North-West, di proliferation of criminal gangs and oda armed groups dey cut off access to grazing reserves and dey fear away potential investors
Unless dem solve am, dis and oda problem fit delay or even scuttle di plan, comot di kontri wey dey vulnerable to di escalation of herder-farmer conflict, wey fit degenerate to wider ethnic, regional and religious violence.
To dey tackle diz challenges require a concerted effort by federal and state authorities, wit help from donors and investors
First, Abuja and supportive state goment suppose provide stronger political leadership and improve public communication to dispel misgiving and misperceptions about di Plan.
Dis dey especially prevalent among pastoralists, wey dem go ask to change a centuries-old nomadic lifestyle, and who legitimately doubt di plan promise about available pasture
But plenti farmers dey fearful too, say dem fit lose land to livestock producers
Many pipo for di Middle Belt and Southern states dey deeply suspicious of di plan long-term goals, wey dem see as creating privileges for herder dem, and more broadly, di Fulani
As Abuja and oda partners dey support, state goment need build expertise and technical capacity, especially for managing ranches and grazing reserves, dairy production and meat processin.
Federal and state goment suppose also up budgetary commitments, hasten di release of funds and provide financial transparency to ensure accountability, work wit donors and investors as necessary
To dey address rural insecurity, curbing impunity and rehabilitating communities wey dey adversely affected by di earlier violence for participatin states dey also important
plus, authorities go need considerate climate change wey fit impact and reach decision about how di transhumant herders wey cross into Nigeria seasonally suppose or suppose no benefit from di plan
While some of dis steps go take time, make di Plan proponent focus on delivering concrete, visible result on a relatively short-term basis.
less than two years from now, di kontri go hold general election
If di plan na to survive di change of goment wey go follow, politicians fit need to dey able to produce clear proof of im benefits (Having served two terms, President Buhari and plenti state governors dey ineligible to compete, meaning say change dey sure.)
Di idea be say, by di time campaignin heats up for late 2022, di Plan backers suppose dey able to point at least a handful of newly constructed ranches or rehabilitated reserves, strong donor and investor commitments, and di first of a cadre of newly trained livestock management professionals.
No one of dis go dey easy, but di plan dey worth di effort
While far from perfect, e dey offer important chance to reform Nigeria livestock system wit strategy wey go address di need of herder dem and small-scale farmers.
Na about 70 per cent of Nigeria workforce dey earn income through agriculture

E-commerce for Nigeria: Legal framework, challenges and prospect
E-commerce refers to di use of communications technology di internet to buy, sell and market goods and services to customers
Di internet don bring about a fundamental shift for national economies wey dey isolated from each oda by barriers to cross-border trade and investment, isolated by distance, time zone and language and isolated by di national differences for goment regulations.
E-commerce dey also promote direct access to distant market dem and dey promote globalisation and commercial activities and dey blur many of di current distinction between domestic and foreign companies to extent say e dey practically impossible to determine di origin of di products.
E-commerce no dey limited to di purchase of product, e include e-mail, and oda communications platforms, all information or service wey a company fit offer to customers ova di net, from pre-purchase information to aftersale service and support.
Di major significance of E-commerce dey for di fact say e dey encourage one single world trading system, wey dem dey facilitate by access through electronic means to goods and services wey kom from different parts of di world
Most of di statues wey dey for Nigeria dey behind towards di development and growth of e-commerce in particular and ICT as a whole
Dem don conduct plenti legal research and study on e-commerce for Nigeria
Most of dis studies dey of di opinion say di Nigerian legal system dey behind ICT-based legislation and say di statutory laws wey dey exist dey quite inadequate to address som of di legal issues wey dey affect e-commerce.
Dis issues include electronic evidence cybercrimes, security and data production, e-payment system and inevitably trans-border issues, and plenti more
There dey, therefore, di need to map out appropriate legal and regulatory measures, to govern di emergein regime.
Dem don tok am well say for Nigeria, significant effort for di regulation of e-commerce-related activities still dey di stage of draft bill before di National Assembly.
E dey important to note say even wia dem pass dis bill to law, Nigeria fit still face new legal issues for dis area wey go call for legislative intervention.
If to say di internet dey permeate commercial transactions for Nigeria, especially for di banking and telecommunications sectors of di economy, di best thing wey we fit suggest na say make dem address di legal issues and problems wey dey face e-commerce for di kontri, straight and quick, for di interest of di teeming population of Nigerian consumers.
To dis, make di National Assembly dey pressureSed into passing bill concerning electronic commerce, a top priority so dat dem go fit enact dem into law as quick as possible.
In di interim, awa court suppose take giant stride if dem interpret di traditional laws wey dey exist liberally so dem go fit bridge di gaps therein in order to make dat kind laws wey dey amenable to electronic transactions, for di emerging trend of di present-day information age.
Indeed, dem argue am say legislation no be di only means wey electronic transaction and attendant documentation fit dey facilitate legally
Di court fit also adapt to recognise electronic transactions in di absence of legislation or private contract
As di then Justice Bingham don put am thus: di common law get for di hand of judges di same facility [as di law merchant] for adaptingsef to di changein needs of di general public; principles no dey alter, but old rules of applying di change, and new rules spring into existence.
Dem dey hope say oda Nigerian Judges, Supreme Court justices inclusive go adopt dis judicial activism, especially for areas wia awa legislation wey dey exist dey grossly inadequate, comprehensive or elaborate enough to address legal challenges of di emerging technological advancements.
Dis go set beta precedence to ensure di protection of e-commerce consumers, entice investors and above all bring about economic development and sustainability for di kontri.
Di germane issues wit regards to contract for E-commerce na documentation and signature
Di provision of section 4 for di Statute of Fraud, 1677 or Lagos State Law Reform (Contract) Law and awa various statutes on top real property and landed instrument, all of dem require make certain contract dey in writing and make dem duly executed
question be say electronic mark or sign constitute valid sign-off of contract by email and e dey binding
Plenti of dis issues still no dey quite settle even for UK although som decision dey incline favourably towards interpreting electronic signature
Marks in emails as e dey satisfy di traditional requirement of writing and due execution
E-commerce transactions na paperless transactions wey dem make through magnetic materials like tapes or disks
Dis dey contradistinction to di paper-based transactions wey dem embodied for permanent form and typically expressed for word and figure usually authenticated by signature
Dem no fit change dat kind transactions witout alteration for di face of di document.
Wit respect to signature, by di provision of section 93(2)(3), evidence act 2011, electronic signature wey dey relate to data message dey conveniently satisfy any requirement for handwritten signature so far e go sufficiently identify electronic record to di individual
Di provision of article 7 of di United Nations Commission on International Trade Law (UNCITRAL) model law on e-commerce 1996 dey equally trite on dis issue
so, for di purpose of establish proof of electronic signature
Di use of a password, identification, user name etc fit suffice

For 2019/2020 wet season, erratic rain for di North and mid-season draught for di South-West make farmers lose dia moni bikos of crop failure
Musa Yohana and Halima Thomas, farmers for Doma and Lafia, Nasarawa State, tok say dem don plant maize, groundnut and oda tuber crop like cassava and yam, but dem dey fear say dem fit get anoda horrible experience like last season wen dem force dem to plant maize and groundnut two times.
Last year, I lose most of my yams bikos of too much heat bikos of inconsistent rainfall.
Dis year, we dey di second week of May but we neva get stable rain
And I don plant 15 measures ( about 20kg) of groundnut already.
If e continue like dis for anoda week, I fit lose di seed, Mr Yohana tok.
Like dem, plenti farmers wey dey around FCT don plant, but di fear of crop failure dey heightened by dia experience for last year
For Benue State, awa correspondent report say farmers don start to dey cultivate dia land and plant crops amid complaints of scanty rainfall
