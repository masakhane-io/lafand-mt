Herder-farmer crisis for Nigeria don bikom dominating topic for all news platforms for Nigeria
End Nigeria herder-farmer crisis: di Livestock Reform Plan
Nigeria federal and state goment dem dey struggle to implement a National Livestock Transformation Plan wey dey seek to stop di movement of cattle across di kontri and reduce herder-farmer conflict wey dey deadly
While di plan don earn di endorsement of plenti state goment, e dey face significant challenge
Deficient political leadership, popular misperception about di purpose, budgetary constraints aggravated by di COVID-19 pandemic, lack of personnel wit di expertise to carry am out and widespread insecurity, dey all hinder progress
If di plan fail as previous initiatives to modernise livestock management do, herder-farmer violence fit escalate
Approved by di National Economic Council for January 2019, di Plan represent di comprehensive effort to overhaul Nigeria inefficient and grossly underperforming livestock system.
Di core na strategy to curtail migration or open grazing, and thus, reduce di risk of conflict wey dey between herder and farmer
E dey animated by di hope say ova 10 years, predominantly nomadic pastoralists go dey persuaded to move dia cattle into ranches and public grazing reserves, wia breedingg farms and oda livestock management practices na to boost di sector's productivity.
By di end of 2028, authorities go get at least 119 ranches wey dey operate for all participatin states, wey go create ova two million jobs for di livestock production, processin and marketing chains.
President Muhammadu Buhari administation kom up wit di Plan afta di wave of violence wey dey between herder dem and farmer dem, a surge for related violence wey dey afflict rural dwellers for 2018
Di federal goment, wey don commit to dey fund 80 per cent of di transformation proposals wey participatin states submit, don take preliminary steps toward puttin di Plan into practice, including to dey provide technical support wey go help states prepare for implementation, like help wit field surveys and site maping.
Plenty states don reactivate earlier demarcated grazing reserves, dey open office dem and dey set up steering committee to administer di Plan
Authorities don also do workshops and do oda works to explain di benefits of livestock reform to large audience
But two years inside di 10-year plan, di first new ranch neva dey build, and di obstacles plenti
Di opposition, partly among herder dem, predominantly from di Fulani ethnic group but also among farmers wey resent di Plan benefits to pastoralists, hinders reform efforts.
Distrust dey widespread for di Middle Belt, wey don be di scene of violent disputes ova land between herder dem and farmer dem, notwitstandin peacemakin and military efforts wey since 2018 don reduce di bloodshed.
Federal and state budget don dey pinched, partly bikos of di pandemic economic fallout, and di kontri no get sufficient technical expertise for managing ranches and grazing reserves.
For many states, especially for di North-West, di proliferation of criminal gangs and oda armed groups dey cut off access to grazing reserves and dey scare away potential investors.
Unless dem solve am, dis and oda problems fit delay or even scuttle di Plan, leaving di kontri vulnerable to an escalation of herder-farmer conflict, wey fit degenerate to wider ethnic, regional and religious violence.
To tackle dis challenges, federal and state authorities go need effort, wit help from dem donor and investor.
First, Abuja and supportive state goment suppose provide stronger political leadership and improve public communication to disple misgiving and misperception about di Plan.
Dis dey especially prevalent among pastoralists, dem go ask dem to change a centuries-old nomadic lifestyle, and dem legitimately doubt di Plan promises about available pastures.
But plenti farmers dey fearful too, say dem fit lose land to livestock producers
Plenti pipo for di Middle Belt and southern states dey deeply suspicious of di plan long-term goal, wey dem see as creating privileges for herder dem, and more broadly, di Fulani
wit Abuja and oda partners support, state goment need build expertise and technical capacity, especially for managing ranches and grazing reserves, dairy production wit meat processin
Federal and state goment suppose dey do budgetary commitments, make dem quick release moni and make dem provide financial transparency wey go ensure say dem dey accountability, dey work wit donors and investors as e dey necessary
To dey address rural insecurity, to dey stop di impunity and to dey rehabilitate communities wey di violence don affect before wen dem dey participate states dey important
plus, authorities go need consider climate change wey fit impact and reach one decision about how di transhumant herder dem wey cross into Nigeria seasonally should or should not benefit from di Plan
While some of dis steps go take time, di Plan proponents suppose focus on delivering concrete, visible result for a relatively short-term basis.
less dan two years now, di kontri go hold general election
If to say di plan na to survive di change of goment wey go follow, politicians fit need to dey able to produce clear proof of hin benefits (Having served two terms, President Buhari and many state governors are not available to compete, meaning say change dey sure.)
ideally, by di time campaignin heats up for late 2022, make di Plan backers dey able to point to at least handful of ranches wey dem just construct or reserves wey dem rehabilitate, strong donor and investor commitments, and di first cadre of livestock management professionals wey dem just train

Wetin not to wear for public: To negate Naija's multiculturalism
By Bashir Ibrahim Hassan
Shey na pure coincidence say, as trouble dey brewing for Kwara State recently, solution to hin kind na hin dey consider for di lower House of di National Assembly wey dey di capital Territory Abuja
Di trouble for Ilorin na becos of di refusal of some Christian faith schools make dem allow dia Muslim student wear hijab go school.
Di solution to dis impasse na di debate on top di Religious Discrimination (Prohibition, Prevention)
RDPP Bill, 2021, derisively tagged Hijab Bill by a section of di Nigerian media
Di two events don cause heated and often emotional debate wey, for di bottom of it all, dey threaten to negate Nigeria multicultural identity and reality
Di idea wey dey Kwara of di proprietors of di schools ban hijab-adorning Muslim student dem and dia sympathisers fit dey reduced to wetin not to wear for public
Dis na di major part of di concern of di RDPP Bill before di House of Representatives di humiliation and denial of opportunities.
female Nigerians dey face wit bikos of wetin dem dey wear for public
Di former dey clearly in breach of di fundamental human right of di Muslim student to show dia faith without discrimination; even though di school fit get dia defence for taking dat kind position.
Di latter dey try assert dis right no be only for Muslims but also Christians wiava or weneva dem dey threatened wit discrimination based on dia faith wey dem professed.
Running through di argument of di proprietors of di christian faith schools for Kwara, one could easily understand di pragmatic appeal of di argument say make Muslim children go oda schools for anoda place if dem no wan conform.
Di problem wey dey get dis argument dey plenti
First, to say Muslim student dem suppose go anoda place unless dey conform to di school rule on uniform dey very small to promote inclusiveness, wey be di bedrock of awa multicultural and multi-religious society
Secondly, di position dey beg di moral question: e dey justifiable to base a school rule bikos of Christian majoritarian nature of di school?
Den, thirdly, di go elsewia argument dey appear to ignore di difficulty to dey change school for pikin
Di inconvenience dey huge
Di act of changing schools for a pikin, educational psychologists go tell you say, e dey interfer negatively wit no be only im emotional but academic development too.
Last last, di Christian faith schools by dem risk wetin dem dey call ghettorization by concentrating students wit di same religious beliefs for particular schools.
Di social cost of dis exacts on di society dey enormous.
Di Commissioner for Education, Kemi Adeosun, argu, wen e dey tok to di press, say di goverment dey convinced say hin policy to allow Muslim schoolgirls wear dia hijab for public schools go lead to sustainable peace and communal harmony anchored on mutual respect and understanding
For a kontri wey guarantee freedom of religion, signed di declaration of International Human Rights, di basic question to ask na: how justifiable is it to deny Nigerian pikin access to school of hin choice on di manifestation of hin religion?
Na big concern bikos Nigeria dey signatory to di Universal Declaration on Human Rights (UDHR).
Di UDHR insist say everybodi dey free and equal, regardless of colour, creed or religion.
E get 30 articles on di right and freedom wey include, among odas, civil and political right like di right to life, free speech and privacy
E also include economic, social and cultural right, like di right to health and education
I find articles 18 and 26 of UDHR wey dey very relevant to di issue under consideration and quote dem for full below:
Article 18: Everybodi get di freedom to dey think or believe wetin dem want, plus di right to religious belief.
We get di right to change awa beliefs or religion for any time, and di right to dey practice awa religion for public or privately, alone or wit odas.
Na evrione get di right to education
Make primary schooling dey free
Make all of us dey able to kontinue awa study as we wish
For school, dem suppose help us develop awa talent, and dem suppose teach us understanding and respect for evribodi's human rights.
Dem suppose teach us make we dey get on top wit odas weda dia ethnicity, religion, or kontri dem wey dem kom from
awa parents get di right to choose which kind school we go to
Dis kind law dem be di instrument wey give Muslim ladies like Ilhan Omar di equality to sit for House of Representatives for di US wey dey wear her Hijab witout any form of molestation or discrimination.
Ilhan Abdullahi Omar na American politician wey be U.S. Representative for Minnesota's 5th congressional district since 2019
Na bill wey di Deputy Chairman of di House of Representatives Committee on Finance and member wey dey represent Bida/Gbako/Katcha Federal Konstituency of Niger State, Hon sponsor
Saidu Musa Abdullahi
Di bill dey seek to address discriminatory conduct and prohibit religious discrimination concerning hiring or recruitment, terms and conditions of employment.
E go prohibit qualifying/professional bodies, educational institutions, employment agencies and oda sectors from discriminating against persins, students or personnel based on dia religious belief or manifestation.
Wen pass, di law go bury issues like wetin we see for Kwara for gud
E go also address di bigotry of some employers, or those executives wey take dia prejudice to public office and allow am becloud dia sense of judgment.
Di law suppose be welcome development
afta all, na only to expand and expatiate di provisions of Naija's constitution and di UDHR document as we explain am above
E dey in line wit similar laws wey dem pass recently for di kontri wey dey seek to tackle social threat and harmful cultural practice di Disability Law and di Violence Against Persins Prohibition (VAPP).
As disability dey seek to end all form of discrimination wey dey base on pesin disabilities, VAPP dey address domestic and oda gender-based violence for di kontri.
One for di time, awa representatives for di legislative chambers dey tackle di social threats wey dey torment awa kontri through legal instrument
Dem dey address dis social ills no be say prior generic law no dey wey go cover dem but bikos dem need to address dem more comprehensively to leave room for opacity
While UDHR fit seem far, distant document wey no get instrument of enforcement, di RDPP Law and odas like am na hin dey provide to allocate punishments for offenders
Contrary to di view of im critic, so far as di RDPP Bill dey concerned, nothing dey to show attempts to seek di enthronement of a state religion for any state wey fit domestice di law eventually.
E no go against Section 10 of di Nigerian Constitution say di goment of di Federation or of a State no go adopt any religion like State Religion.
To peddle dis argument, bikos di bill dey call on di general public make dem respect di rights of Muslim women wey dey wear Hijab for public spaces smacks of Islamophobia only
For di final analysis, to deny a Nigerian male or female hin or her religious freedom negate awa multicultural pretenses
E negate di concept of equality, and basic human right as e dey for inside Nigeria's constitution and di Universal Declaration of Human Rights legal document.
Di bill wey go criminalize dis kind decimations based on religious grounds, like di RDPP bill, na welcome development

E-commerce for Nigeria: Legal framework, challenges and prospect
E-commerce dey reflect to dey use of communications technology di internet to buy, sell and market goods and services to customers
Di internet don bring about a fundamental shift in national economies wey dey isolated from each oda by barriers to cross-border trade and investment, isolated by distance, time zone and language and isolated by di national differences in goment regulations.
E-commerce dey also promote direct access to distant markets and promote globalisation and commercial activities and e dey blur many of di current distinction wey dey between domestic and foreign companies to extent say e no dey possible to determine di origin of di product
E-commerce no dey limited to di purchase of product, e include e-mail, and oda communications platform, all information or service wey a company fit offer to customers ova di net, from pre-purchase information to aftersale service and support.
Di major significance of E-commerce dey for di fact say e dey encourage one single world trading system, wey dem dey facilitate by access through electronic means to goods and services wey kom from different parts of di world
Most of di statues wey dey exist for Nigeria dey behind towards di development and growth of e-commerce in particular and ICT as a whole
Dem don do plenti legal research and study on e-commerce for Nigeria
Most of dis tins be say di opinion of di Nigerian legal system lag for behind ICT-based legislation and say di statutory laws wey dey exist dey quite inadequate to address some of di legal issues wey dey affect e-commerce.
Dis issues include electronic evidence cybercrimes, security and data production, e-payment system and inevitably trans-border issues, and many more.
There are, therefore, di need to map out appropriate legal and regulatory measures, to govern di emerging regime.
Dem don tok am well well say for Nigeria, significant effort for di regulation of e-commerce-related activities still dey di stage of draft bill before di National Assembly.
E dey important to note say even for wia dem pass dis bills into law, Nigeria fit still face new legal issues for dis area wey go call for legislative intervention.
If to say di speed wey Internet dey use pass commercial transactions for Nigeria, especially for di banking and telecommunication sectors of di economy, di best tin wey we fit suggest na say make dem solve di legal issues and problems wey dey face e-commerce for di kontri, directly and quick, wit di interest of di teeming population of Nigerian consumers.
To dis one, make dem pressure di National Assembly to pass bill concerning electronic commerce, wey be di top priority so dem go fit enact dem into law as quick as possible
In di interim, awa court suppose take giant stride by interpreting di traditional laws wey dey exist liberally so as to bridge di gap derin in order to make dat kind laws amenable to electronic transactions, especially for di emerging trend of di present-day information age.
Indeed, dem argue am say legislation no be di only means wey dem fit use electronic transaction and attendant documentation legally
Di court fit also adapt to recognise electronic transactions if legislation or private contract no dey.
As di then Justice Bingham don put am so: di common law get di same facility for judges hand as di law merchant) as dem dey adapt demsef to di changein need of di general public; principles no dey alter, but old rules wey dey apply to di change, and new rules wey start to existence.
Dem dey hope say oda Nigerian Judges go adopt dis judicial activism, Supreme Court justice inclusive, especially for areas wia awa current legislation dey grossly inadequate, comprehensive or elaborate enough to address legal challenges of di emerging technological advancements.
Dis go set good precedence to ensure di protection of e-commerce consumers, entice investors and above all, e go bring about economic development and sustainability for di kontri.
Di germane issues wit regards to contracts for E-commerce na documentation and signature
The provision of section 4 for di Statute of fraud, 1677 or Lagos State Law Reform (Contract) Law and our various statutes on real property and instrument wey dem land, all of dem go need make sure say some contract dey write and make dem execute well. The
question be say electronic mark or sign constitute valid sign-off of contract by email and be binding
Plenti of dis issues still no dey quite settle even for UK although som decision dey favourable towards interpreting electronic signature
so mark for e-mails as dey satisfy di traditional requirement of writing and due execution
E-commerce transactions na paperless transactions wey dem make through magnetic materials like tapes or discks.
Dis dey in contradistinction to di paper-based transactions wey dem embodied for permanent form and typically expressed for word and figure usually authenticated by signature
We no fit alter dat kind transactions witout an alteration for di face of di document.
wit respect to signatures, by di provision of section 93(2)(3)), evidence act 2011, electronic signature wey dey relate to data message conveniently satisfy any requirement for handwritten signature so far e reach to identify electronic record to di persin
Di provision of article 7 of di United Nations Commission on International Trade Law (UNCITRAL) model law on e-commerce 1996 dey equally trite on dis issue
so, for di purpose of establish proof of electronic signatures

Farmers dey apprehensive as planting season start wit erratic rain
As wet season dey start for most part of di kontri, farmers don start, plant, albeit wit fear afta erratic rain wey fit threaten production
Di rain don dey unstable for Benue, Nasarawa, Niger, Kaduna and parts of di Federal Capital Territory (FCT), and plenti farmers don plant dia crops
For di 2019/2020 wet season, erratic rain for di North and mid-season draught for di South-West make farmers lose dia moni bikos of crop failure
Musa Yohana and Halima Thomas, farmers for Doma and Lafia, Nasarawa State, tok say dem don plant maize, groundnut and oda tuber crop like cassava and yam, but dem fear say dem fit get anoda horrible experience like last season wen dem force dem to plant maize and groundnut two times.
I lose most of my yams last year bikos of too much heat bikos of inconsistent rainfall.
Dis year, we dey di second week of may but we neva get stable rain
And I don plant 15 measures (about 20kg) of groundnut already
Mr Yohana tok say if e continue like dis for anoda week, I fit lose di seeds.
Like dem, plenti farmers wey dey around FCT don plant, but di fear of crop failure don heightened by dia experience for last year
For Benue State, awa correspondent report say farmers don begin dey cultivate dia land and plant crop amid complaints of scanty rainfall
Some farmers wey don prepare dia farm early for di year tok say dem no fit start plantin crop until one week ago, while odas just start to till dia land for di wake of rainfall (thrice) within di past two weeks.
One farmer for Otukpo, Ada Hyacinth tok say di ground too strong to cultivate bikos di rain neva dey frequent
anoda farmer wey be Felix Tor for Makurdi tok say hin just plant melon and maize for hin farm wey hin just plougged despite di inconsistentcy of di rain
Tor, howeva, don express fear say di kind rain fall fit affect di well-being of di crop
E pray make di yield turn out gud for di end
But Vitalis Tarnongu, wey dey plough hin 1,000 hectares of farmland for di Federal University of Agriculture, Makurdi (FUAM), posite say di manner of rainfall dis year go require am to begin dey plant maize towards di end of may
Tarnongu tok say a plant breeder for di university don advise am make hin wait until May 20 to begin dey plant hin maize bikos di nature of di rainfall since di season set in suggest say e fit be two weeks drought.
Aondongu Saaku wey be di state chairman of All Farmers Association of Nigeria (AFAN) describe farmin as business wey worth taking risk on
Saaku tok say farmers wey see am as business don take di risk to plant dia crop bikos if dem no see dat kind risk, dem no go reap at di end of di day.
Meanwhile, Dr Teryima Iorlamen, wey be seed system specialist for di FUAM, tok say farmers fit go ahead to plant dia root and tuber crop in line wit di NIMET rainfall prediction for dis year
Iorlamen tok say di period wey di farmers suppose wait awhile before planting don go, tok say di NIMET prediction for rainfall pattern for Benue State, wey dem (exper dem) work wit, show say di rain go stabilise for di state between April 1830 and 2021.
