End Nigeria herder-farmer crisis: di Livestock Reform Plan
Nigerias federal and state goment dey struggle to implement a National Livestock Transformation Plan wey wan curb di movement of cattle across di kontri and reduce deadly herder-farmer conflict
While di Plan don get di endorsement of plenti state goment, e dey face significant challenge
Deficient political leadership, popular misperceptions about hin purpose, budgetary constraints wey di COVID-19 pandemic aggravate, lack of personnel wey get di expertise wey go carry am out and insecurity dey widespread, dey hinder progress
If di Plan fail as previous initiatives to modernise livestock management don do, herder-farmer violence fit escalate
Wey di National Economic Council approve for January 2019, di Plan represent di comprehensive effort to overhaul Nigeria inefficient and grossly underperforming livestock system.
For di core na strategy to curtail migratory or open grazing, and thus, reduce di risk of conflict wey dey between herder and farmers
Dem animate am wit di hope say ova 10 years, dem go persuade nomadic pastoralists to move dia cattle into ranches and public grazing reserves, wia breeding farms and oda mechanized livestock management practices na to boost di productivity of di sector.
By di end of 2028, authorities dey aim to get at least 119 ranches wey dey operate for all participating states, wey dey create ova two million jobs for di livestock production, processin and marketing chains.
President Muhammadu Buhari administration kom up wit di plan afta a wave of violence wey dey between herders and farmers, a rise of violence wey dey related affect rural dwellers for 2018
Di federal goment, wey don commit to fund 80 per cent of di transformation proposals wey participating states submit, don take preliminary steps toward putin di Plan into practice, plus provide technical support wey go help states prepare for implementation, like help wit field surveys and site maping.
Several states don reactivate earlier demarcated grazing reserves, office wey dem open and set up steering committees wey go manage di plan
Authorities don also hold workshops and dem do oda works to explain di benefits of livestock reform to a larger audience.
But two years inside di 10-year plan, dem neva build di first new ranch, and di obstacle dey plenti
Di opposition, partly among herders, mainly from di Fulani ethnic group but also among farmers wey resent di Plans benefit to pastoralists, dey hinder reform effort
distrust dey widespread for di Middle Belt, wey don be di scene of violent disputes ova land wey dey between herders and farmers, even as dem dey make peace and military effort wey since 2018 don reduce di bloodshed.
Federal and state budget dey pinched, partly bikos of di pandemic economic fallout, and di kontri no get enough technical expertise to manage ranches and grazing reserves.
For many states, especially for di North-West, di proliferation of criminal gangs and oda armed groups dey cut off access to grazing reserves and dey scare away potential investors
Unless dem solve, dis and oda problems fit delay or even scuttle di Plan, wey go make di kontri vulnerable to escalation of herder-farmer conflict, wey fit degenerate into wider ethnic, regional and religious violence.
To dey tackle dis challenges require concerted effort by federal and state authorities, wit help from donors and investors.
First, Abuja and supportive state goment suppose provide stronger political leadership and improve public communication to dispel misgivings and misperceptions about di Plan.
Dis dey especially prevalent among pastoralists, wey dem go ask make dem change one nomadic lifestyle wey be century old, and wey legitimately doubt di Plans promise about di pasture wey dey available
But plenti farmers dey fearful too, say dem fit lose land to livestock producers
Many for Middle Belt and Southern states dey deeply suspicious of di Plans long-term goals, wey dem see as creating privileges for herders, and more widely, di Fulani.
wit Abuja and oda partners support, state goment must build expertise and technical capacity, especially for managing ranches and grazing reserves, dairy production and meat processing
Federal and state goment suppose also up budgetary commitment, accelerate di release of funds and provide financial transparency to ensure accountability, work wit donors and investors as necessary.
To dey address rural insecurity, to dey prevent impunity and to dey rehabilitate communities wey previous violence affect dey important for participating states
plus, authorities go need consider climate change wey fit impact and make dem reach a decision about how di transhumant herders wey cross into Nigeria seasonally suppose or make dem no benefit from di Plan.
While some of dis steps go take time, di Plans proposers suppose focus on to deliver concrete, visible result on a relatively short-term basis.
less dan two years from now, di kontri go hold general election
If di plan na to survive di change of goment wey go follow, politicians fit need to be able to produce clear proof of hin benefits (Having served two terms, President Buhari and plenti state governors are ineligible to compete, meaning say change dey sure.)
ideally, by di time wey campaign dey hot for end 2022, di Plans supporters suppose dey able to point to at least a handful of newly constructed ranches or rehabilitated reserves, strong donor and investor commitment, and di first cadre of newly trained livestock management professionals.
No one of dis go dey easy, but di Plan dey worth di effort
As e no dey perfect, e dey offer important opportunity to reform Nigeria livestock system wit strategy wey go address di needs of both herder and small-scale farmers
About 70 per cent of Nigeria's workforce dey earn income through agriculture

E-commerce for Nigeria: Legal framework, challenge and prospect
E-commerce dey refer to di use of communication technology di internet to buy, sell and market goods and services to customers.
Di internet don bring about a fundamental shift for national economies wey dey isolated from each oda by barriers to cross-border trade and investment, isolated by distance, time zone and languages and isolated by di national difference for goment regulations.
E-commerce sef dey promote direct access to distant market and e dey promote globalisation and commercial activities and dey blur many of di current distinction wey dey between domestic and foreign companies to extent say e no dey possible to determine di origin of di products.
E-commerce no dey limited to di purchase of product, e include e-mail, and oda communication platforms, all information or service wey company fit offer to customer ova di net, from pre-purchase information to aftersale service and support
Di major significance of e-commerce na for di fact say e dey encourage one single world trading system, wey dem dey facilitate by access through electronic means to goods and services wey kom from different parts of di world
Most of di statues wey dey exist for Nigeria dey back towards di development and growth of e-commerce in particular and ICT as a whole.
Dem don do plenti legal research and study on e-commerce for Nigeria
Most of dis studies dey of di opinion say di Nigerian legal system dey behind ICT-based legislation and say di statutory law wey dey exist dey quite inadequate to address some of di legal issues wey dey affect e-commerce.
Dis issues include electronic evidence cybercrime, security and data production, e-payment system and inevitably trans-border issues, and plenti more.
There dey, therefore, di need to map out appropriate legal and regulatory measures, to govern di emerging regime.
Dem don tok am seriously say for Nigeria, significant effort for di regulation of e-commerce-related activities still dey di stage of Draft Bill before di National Assembly.
E dey important to note say, even wia dem pass dis bills into law, Nigeria fit still face new legal issues for dis area wey go call for legislative intervention.
Sake of di speed wey di internet dey permeate commercial transactions for Nigeria, especially for di banking and telecommunications sector of di economy, di best wey dem fit suggest be say di legal issues and problems wey dey present face e-commerce for di kontri suppose dey address directly and speedyly, for di interest of di population of Nigerian consumers.
To dis one, make dem pressure di National Assembly make dem pass bills wey dey relate to electronic commerce, one priority make dem fit enact dem into law as soon as possible.
For di time, awa court suppose take big strike as dem dey interpret di traditional laws wey dey exist freely so dem go fit bridge di gap wey dey there so dem go fit make dat kind laws available for electronic transactions, especially for di trend wey dey emerge for di present-day information age.
Indeed, dem argue say legislation no be di only means wey e fit facilitate electronic transactions and documentation wey dey attend legally.
Di courts fit adapt to recognise electronic transactions in the absence of legislation or private contract.
As di Justice Bingham don put am one time: di common law get for di hand of judges di same facility wey dem go fit adapt to di changing needs of di general public; principles no dey change, but old rules wey go dey apply di change, and new rules don spring into existence.
Dem dey hope say oda Nigerian judges go adopt dis judiciary activism, including Supreme Court justices, especially for areas wey awa current legislation dey grossly inadequate, comprehensive or elaborate enough to address legal challenges of di emerging technological advances.
Dis go set a good precedent to ensure di protection of e-commerce consumers, entice investors and above all bring about economic development and sustainability for di kontri.
Di German issue wey relate to contract for e-commerce na documentation and signature
Di provision of section 4 of di Statute of Fraud, 1677 or Lagos State Law Reform (Contact) Law and awa various statutes on top real property and landed instrument, wey all require som contract make dem write and make dem do am well
di question be say electronic mark or sign go constitute valid sign-off of contract by email and e go dey binding
Many of dis issues still no dey solve well even for UK although some decision dey favourably towards interpreting electronic signature
so e mark for email as satisfying di traditional requirement of writing and due execution
E-commerce transactions na paperless transactions wey dem do through magnetic materials like tapes or disks
Dis dey contrary to di paper-based transactions wey dey embodied in permanent form and dey typically expressed in words and figures wey dey usually authenticated by signatures
Dem no fit change dat kind transactions witout make dem change di face of di document.
Regarding di signature, by di provision of Section 93(2)(3), evidence act 2011, an electronic signature wey dey related to data message conveniently meet any requirement for handwritten signature so tey e dey sufficiently identify an electronic record to di individual.

Musa Yohana and Halima Thomas, farmers for Doma and Lafia, Nasarawa State, tok say dem don plant maize, groundnut and oda tuber crops like cassava and yam, but dem fear say dem fit get anoda horrible experience like last season wen dem force dem to plant maize and groundnut two times.
I lose most of my yams last year bikos of excessive heat bikos of rainfall wey no dey consistent
Dis year, we dey for di second week of May but we neva get stable rain
And I don plant 15 measures (about 20kg) of groundnut already
Mr Yohana tok say if e continue like dis for anoda week, I fit lose di seed
Like dem, plenti farmers wey dey around FCT don plant, but di fear of crop failure don increase by dia experience of last year
For Benue State, awa correspondent report say farmers don start to cultivate dia land and plant crops amidst complaint of scanty rainfall
